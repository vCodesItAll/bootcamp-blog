Halfway

I am now halfway through bootcamp. The biggest hurdle has been handling the emotions. My happiness depends on my cohort's success. If a member is down I feel down and I hate to see my classmates hit blockers or feel not confident. In contrast, the online web development class was much easier because I only had to rally myself. I think keeping people's spirits high as the holidays draw near and our final project approaches will be the final boss. I'm hoping the Codey Awards will instill confidence in the class and give us the driving push to end this class on a high note.

I want my final project to be a resource for domestic violence victims. Perhaps an application that allows victims to access information in secret, or a way to communicate for help without alerting their aggressor, or a way to create a secret network for victims to identify abusers in the community without retaliation. These are the same ideas I've had in mine from the start as they are my driving "why" for being here. Coding is not my passion but bringing abusers to justice and affording aid to victims is.

I'm surprised by how easy my first week with Python feels after React. The katas are a breeze again. There's less complex syntax. It feels more at home than the first week of javascript felt. I'm learning Python now in a much better classroom environment. It's been a blast and I'm itching to incorporate it into the next class project.
